What is Vedic Numerology?

Vedic Numerology — known in ancient Sanskrit texts as Ankaśāstra — is one of the oldest divinatory sciences rooted in Jyotish. Your date of birth holds the immutable seed of your cosmic blueprint.

🌙 Moolank — The Root Number

Your Moolank is calculated by reducing your birth day to a single digit (1–9). It reveals your core nature, ruled by a Vedic planet: Sun, Moon, Jupiter, Rahu, Mercury, Venus, Ketu, Saturn, or Mars.

🌟 Bhagyank — The Destiny Number

Summing your full date of birth (DD+MM+YYYY) gives your Bhagyank. It signifies your life path and karmic destiny.

🕉️

Example (Narendra Modi, 17 Sep 1950): Moolank 8 (Saturn), Bhagyank 5 (Mercury). The grid shows amplified 8 (perseverance) and 5 (communication).

📜 Mahadasha & Antardasha

Mahadasha cycles through nine planets starting from your Moolank. Antardasha is computed yearly: Year# + Moolank + birth month + weekday number, reduced to a single digit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Vedic Numerology same as Pythagorean?

No. Vedic numerology is birth-date based, uses Mahadasha/Antardasha cycles and a distinct planetary grid.

Can Moolank and Bhagyank be the same?

Yes, it aligns inner nature with destiny, creating clear purpose.
