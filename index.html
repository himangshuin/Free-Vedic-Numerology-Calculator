Understanding Vedic Numerology: A Complete Guide

Vedic Numerology, also known as Indian numerology, is an ancient system derived from the Vedas. It assigns numerical values to birth dates to reveal personality traits, life purpose, and karmic patterns. The Vedic system focuses heavily on the Moolank (Root Number) and Bhagyank (Destiny Number) calculated from your date of birth.

🌙 What is Moolank (Root Number)?

Your Moolank is the single-digit sum of your day of birth. For example, born on the 23rd → 2+3=5. This represents your innate nature, mental tendencies, and how others perceive you.

🌟 Bhagyank (Destiny Number)

Bhagyank is calculated by summing all digits of your full date of birth (DD+MM+YYYY) and reducing to a single digit. This reveals your life path and major life events.

🕉️ The Sacred Vedic Grid (3x3 Chakra)

The Vedic grid is a 3x3 mystical square with fixed positions: top row (3,1,9), middle row (6,7,5), bottom row (2,8,4). By mapping digits from your birth date plus Moolank and Bhagyank, we create a personalized energetic map.

🪐 Mahadasha & Antardasha: Planetary Periods

Mahadasha cycles start from your Moolank following the order: 1(Sun),2(Moon),3(Jupiter),4(Rahu),5(Mercury),6(Venus),7(Ketu),8(Saturn),9(Mars). Each lasts for years equal to its number.

Antardasha (Corrected Method): For each calendar year after birth, we take the Year Number = last two digits of the year (e.g., 2018 → 18, 2025 → 25). Then add: YearNumber + Root Number + Birth Month Number + Day-of-Week Number (where Monday=2, Tuesday=9, Wednesday=5, Thursday=3, Friday=6, Saturday=8, Sunday=1). Reduce the sum to a single digit to get the Antardasha planet for that year.

Example (Narendra Modi, 17/09/1950): For calendar year 2018 → Year Number 18 + Root(8) + Month(9) + Day(2 for Monday) = 37 → 1 → Sun.
